Yan Ding

As a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the State University of New York (SUNY) at Binghamton, I am supervised by Assistant Professor Shiqi Zhang and supported by grants from the Ford Motor Company. My research focus is on the field of Robotics, specifically in the area of Task and Motion Planning. My current interests lie in three specific areas within this domain:

1) The planning problem, specifically the trade-off between safety (at the task level) and efficiency (at the motion level). 2) The grounding problem, which deals with how to plan when object properties are unknown. 3) Handling unforeseen situations in open-world scenarios, such as how to react to a broken cup when the task is to deliver a cup of water.
